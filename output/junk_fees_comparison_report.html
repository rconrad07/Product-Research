Total Fees & Taxes Display Comparison

Analysis of Junk Fee Transparency across Vacation Ownership Leaders

Executive Summary

Following the enactment of California's SB 478 and the FTC's Junk Fees Rule, hospitality companies have shifted toward "upfront total pricing." This analysis compares how four leading vacation ownership brands—Hilton Grand Vacations, Marriott Vacations, Disney Vacations Club, and Starwood (Vistana)—have implemented these changes to improve the booking experience.

Law & Regulatory Context

As of 2024, US-based lodging providers are increasingly required to show all mandatory fees (resort fees, cleaning fees, etc.) at the first price display. While taxes are occasionally permitted to be added later (depending on state law), the industry trend is toward "Total Price" transparency.

Detailed Analysis

Marriott Vacations Club

Excellent Transparency

Following a 2021 settlement, Marriott (including MVC) became the gold standard for "total pricing." Their UI typically shows the full stay cost including resort fees as the primary figure, eliminating sticker shock during the final booking steps.

Disney Vacations Club

User-Centric (Fee Minimalist)

DVC's "user friendliness" comes from its business model: they rarely charge separate resort fees. Instead, they provide explicit tax lookup tables for regions like Anaheim and Hawaii, ensuring point-based users are never surprised by per-night taxes.

Hilton Grand Vacations

High Compliance (Toggle Based)

HGV uses a modern "Total Price" toggle. While the default can sometimes be nightly, the breakdown of taxes and fees is just one click away and very granular, reflecting a high commitment to recent transparency laws.

Starwood (Vistana)

Improving Consistency

Vistana leverages the Marriott engine. Many properties, such as Sheraton Vistana, actively market "No Extra Resort Fees" as a competitive advantage. This marketing-focused transparency is highly effective for conversion.

Strategic Recap

To maximize user friendliness, companies should follow the Marriott Model of presenting a single, all-in price by default. Disney remains the leader in trust by avoiding junk fees entirely, while Hilton’s toggle approach offers flexibility but slightly more friction than Marriott’s "Inclusive Default."
